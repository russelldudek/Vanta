01 / The actual work

Turn fast ideas into trusted operating infrastructure.

The role sits inside Recruiting and People, but its real work is product-like: discover friction, choose the right mechanism, build quickly, preserve human judgment, integrate with system boundaries, document the pattern, drive adoption, and keep it healthy after launch.

Builder speed × durability

Rapid prototypes matter only when they become owned, documented, observable workflows—not permanent founderware.

Automation × human authority

Candidate communication and People decisions demand explicit lines between assistance, execution, approval, and accountability.

Embedded autonomy × architecture

The partner must ship without a full engineering team while respecting People Systems, data structures, access, and source-of-truth boundaries.

Tool exploration × coherence

Dust, Claude, Zapier, scripts, and integrations must serve one operating model rather than become a scattered collection of experiments.

Hiring velocity × candidate trust

Efficiency cannot come at the cost of context, transparency, exception handling, or a candidate experience that feels automated in the wrong moments.

Launch volume × maintenance capacity

Every new workflow creates an ownership and upkeep obligation. The portfolio needs clear decisions to standardize, repair, retire, or leave human-led.

03 / Operating model

A lightweight warranty for every workflow.

The framework keeps tool choice subordinate to the work. It supports Vanta’s bias for action while making durability, judgment, and maintenance explicit.

01

Read the work

Observe the actual handoffs, exceptions, knowledge gaps, data boundaries, and user burden—not just the stated request.

02

Choose the mechanism

Use process redesign, deterministic automation, scripts/integrations, or AI according to the work’s variability and risk.

03

Bound authority

Declare what the workflow may act on, what a human must approve, what evidence is required, and when it must stop.

04

Ship a thin slice

Prototype the smallest useful path inside a real pod, with visible assumptions, exception handling, and owner participation.

05

Verify in use

Measure labor avoided, cycle time, reliability, candidate or employee experience, recovery, and retained adoption.

06

Maintain or retire

Document the pattern, assign stewardship, review knowledge freshness, manage changes, and remove workflows that no longer earn their upkeep.

The value proposition

  • Builds directly in workflows and tools rather than stopping at strategy.
  • Distinguishes AI from standard automation, scripts, and process redesign.
  • Designs for ownership, exception recovery, learning, and adoption.
  • Can partner across nontechnical users, systems owners, technical teams, and executives.
  • Uses evidence boundaries honestly and makes uncertainty visible.
05 / Balanced scorecard

Measure the work, not the novelty.

No unsupported ROI promise. Each workflow receives a baseline, units, owner, and review cadence before a target is treated as real.

Hiring flow

Time-to-schedule, coordination touches, exception age, candidate response latency, handoff completeness.

Reliability

Failure rate, recovery time, stale knowledge, duplicate actions, unowned exceptions, reconciliation errors.

Experience

Recruiter effort, hiring-manager clarity, candidate confusion, employee friction, trust-sensitive escalations.

Adoption

Retained usage, bypass behavior, edit/rework patterns, pod spread, champion participation, training burden.

Economics

Labor hours avoided, cycle-time reduction, vendor/tool cost, maintenance load, reuse across workflows.

Learning

Time from idea to bounded test, decision speed, documented patterns, retired automations, reusable skills and connectors.

06 / First 90 days

Read deeply. Ship narrowly. Scale what survives contact with work.

Days 1–30

Map and baseline

  • Read Recruiting and People work across pods.
  • Inventory automations, agents, ownership, knowledge, and failure modes.
  • Define a thin warranty standard and opportunity scorecard.
  • Select one high-value Recruiting workflow and one People workflow.
Days 31–60

Build and verify

  • Ship bounded production slices with named owners and fallbacks.
  • Establish Ashby-first automation patterns and Dust/Claude boundaries.
  • Instrument workflow behavior, exception recovery, and user adoption.
  • Document reusable skills, connectors, and launch checklists.
Days 61–90

Standardize and spread

  • Review evidence and decide scale, repair, keep human-led, or retire.
  • Create a portfolio cadence with Recruiting, People Ops, and People Systems.
  • Publish a maintained knowledge architecture and change protocol.
  • Teach the team through demos, office hours, and reusable examples.
